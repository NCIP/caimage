Image Here Description: (Slide# 29999A) This slide contains the spleen (x=11373 y=19004 l=20) and liver with dark perivascular patches (x=19641 y=17456 l=15). The spleen is identified by the fibrous capsule and scattered myeloid cells and megakaryocytes (x-28110 y=16977 l=1). The normal architecture is replaced by a peri-PAL infiltrate of neoplastic lymphocytes with scattered clear macrophages imparting a starry sky appearance to the neoplasm (x=6226 y=7067 l=3.3). On close inspection, the neoplastic cells have large pale nuclei with relatively abundant cytoplasm and the macrophages have many tingible bodies (x=5847 y=7184 l=1).

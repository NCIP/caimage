Help for Submission

IntroductionWe would like to give you a short introduction before you start submitting a images to caImage, the Cancer Images Database.
Alternatively, you can take the "Guided Tour".
Required FieldsIt is required to include the following information for each image:
- Species
- Organ/Tissue (select from controlled vocabulary)
- Image Name
- Uploading of the Image
SpeciesSelect species from the drop down list. Depending on your selection the controlled vocabularies for organ/tissue and diagnosis are chosen. Please make sure that you select Species first before you fill in any of the other fields.
Organ/TissueWhen you click the "Select" button next to the Organ/Tissue field a new browser window will open. Depending on your selection in the species drop-down list the vocabularies are populated. The vocabularies are presented in a tree-structure. Clicking on the folders will open them up and allow access to sub-terms. A term is selected by clicking on the hyperlinked name.
You can also search for an organ/tissue by entering the word in the search field provided at the top left corner. The search results can be displayed in the vocabulary tree or directly selected by clicking on the hyperlinked name.
Once you selected an organ/tissue it will be inserted in the corresponding field on the submission page and the window with the vocabulary tree will close automatically.
DiagnosisWhen you click the "Select" button next to the Diagnosis field a new browser window will open and diagnoses specific for the selected organ will be displayed.
Clicking on the folders will open them up and allow access to sub-terms. A term is selected by clicking on the hyperlinked name.
Searching for a diagnosis pertaining to a specific organ
You can also search for a diagnosis by entering the word in the search field provided at the top left corner. The search results can be displayed in the vocabulary tree or directly selected by clicking on the hyperlinked name. Please be aware that the search is only conducted in the part of the tree that is displayed on the right site. Once you select an organ/tissue it will be inserted in the corresponding field on the submission page and the window with the vocabulary tree will close automatically.
Diagnosis not found. What next?
If the manual search or automatic search did not retrieve the desired result you can display all diagnoses in the vocabulary. To search the complete vocabulary click on the "Show all diagnoses" arrow and search again by entering the word in the search field on the top left corner. The vocabularies are are structured by "Diagnoses per Organ". Searching the complete diagnosis tree may return many results especially when you search for terms like "adenocarcinoma", because this particular kind of tumor can arise in many organs. Verify the diagnosis by viewing it in the data tree. Once you select an organ/tissue it will be inserted in the corresponding field on the submission page and the window with the vocabulary tree will close automatically.
Diagnosis still not found? Entering a new diagnostic term.
If none of the previously described searches retrieves a matching diagnostic term you can manually enter a diagnosis. A "Submit" button in the left bottom frame opens a new window which allow you to enter the missing term. This term will be forwarded to members of the vocabulary team who decide if this diagnosis will be included in the vocabulary.
Normal tissue
Currently, the vocabulary does not contain information for normal tissue. Use the Image Description field to indicate that the image was taken from non-diseased tissue.
GenderSelect a value from the drop-down list.
Mouse StrainSelect a strain from the drop-down list.
PromoterThis field applies only to genetically engineered rodents. If more than one promoter was used (e.g. in a double transgenic mouse) separate your entries by comma.
GeneThis field applies only to genetically engineered rodents. If more than one gene was altered/inserted (e.g. in a double transgenic mouse) separate your entries by comma.
PMIDThe PubMed identifier (PMID) is a unique number given to every publication listed in PubMed. Look up the PMID by clicking on the PubMed logo.
Image ModalityRight now the only choice is Histology. We will add soon more modalities.
Image NameGive your image a short but descriptive name, e.g. mammary adenocarcinoma of MMTV/neu mouse
Image DescriptionThe image description can contain information on
- diseases
- cellular structures
- specific point of interest on the image
- the imaging technology used
- magnification
- animal model
StainingSelect a value from the drop-down list.
Image UploadClick the "Browse" button, navigate to the image on your hard drive or other storage device, highlight the image and click the "Open" button.
Submitting Image and DataAfter you hit the submit button on the bottom of the page the transfer of data and images begins. Depending on the size of the image this might take one minute or longer. Please be patient and do not click the submit button again. This will disrupt the submission and the data might get lost.
Once image and data have been stored on the caImage server the application opens a new page.
